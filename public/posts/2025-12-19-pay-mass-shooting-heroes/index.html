Governments Should Pay People Who Stop Mass Shootings

December 19, 2025

The article proposes that governments should financially reward civilians who successfully stop mass shootings. The author uses the December 2025 Bondi Beach shooting in Australia as a case study, where an unarmed bystander named Ahmed al-Ahmed tackled one of the gunmen, potentially preventing additional deaths.

The Monetary Value Framework

Governments already assign dollar values to human lives for policy decisions. Australia values a statistical life at 5.7 million AUD, while U.S. agencies range from 7.5 to 13+ million USD.

The Proposal

The author suggests that Ahmed likely saved approximately 16 lives based on statistical analysis of mass shooting distributions. Using government valuations, this would justify a reward around 91.2 million AUD (60 million USD).

Counterarguments Addressed

The author anticipates objections: encouraging reckless behavior, implementation challenges, and whether resources should target other causes. He acknowledges being “at most 60% sure” the proposal benefits society, noting safety guidance typically recommends avoidance rather than confrontation.

Broader Context

The piece connects to the author’s interest in outcome-based philanthropy and aligning incentives. He argues this demonstrates how governments could directly reward measurable life-saving interventions.
